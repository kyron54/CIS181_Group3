The Ultimate Endurance Challenge

The Ironman triathlon (2.4-mile swim, 112-mile bike, 26.2-mile run) represents the pinnacle of endurance sports. Conquering this event demands not just physical strength, but also mental fortitude, strategic planning, and disciplined training across three distinct disciplines.

Comprehensive Multisport Training

Our Ironman training plan is designed to transform dedicated athletes into complete triathletes capable of conquering the full 140.6 miles. This program blends swimming, cycling, running, and strength training into a progressive framework that builds endurance while preventing injury.

This 24-30 week program accommodates athletes coming from different backgrounds, whether you're a strong runner looking to improve your swim, or a cyclist building running endurance. The plan includes periodization to peak at the right time for your A-race.

How to Train for an Ironman (24-30 Weeks Plan)

Your Weekly Structure

  • 9-12 training sessions per week (3 swims, 3-4 bikes, 3 runs, 1-2 strength)
  • Long swims building to 4000 yards/meters
  • Long rides extending to 100+ miles
  • Long runs building to 18-20 miles
  • 1-2 recovery days per week

Key Workouts

  1. Swim Technique Sessions: Focus on efficiency and form with drills and interval work.
  2. Long Brick Workouts: Bike-to-run transitions to train your legs for the specific demands of triathlon.
  3. Progressive Long Rides: Building both duration and intensity to simulate race conditions.
  4. Tempo Runs: Mid-distance runs at moderate-to-hard effort to build running efficiency.
  5. Race Simulation Days: Practicing nutrition, pacing, and transitions in conditions similar to race day.

Race Strategy & Execution

The Swim (2.4 miles)

Start Position: Position yourself based on your realistic swim time. Unless you're a front-pack swimmer, consider starting wide or hanging back 5-10 seconds to avoid the chaos.

Pacing: The swim is about 10% of your day but can drain 40% of your energy if done poorly. Stay relaxed, find your rhythm, and swim within yourself.

The Bike (112 miles)

Power Management: Stay 10-15% below your FTP for most of the ride. The first 50 miles should feel easyâ€”that's intentional. The race doesn't begin until the run.

Nutrition: Consume 60-90g carbs per hour and stay on top of hydration and electrolytes. The bike is where you fuel for the entire race.

The Run (26.2 miles)

Start Conservative: Begin at a pace that feels too easy. The first 10K will dictate your marathon.

Break It Down: Don't think about 26.2 miles. Focus on reaching the next aid station, which comes every mile.

Problem Solving: Every Ironman run includes rough patches. Walk aid stations if needed, reset your nutrition, and keep moving forward.

Nutrition Strategy

Pre-race

Wake up 3+ hours before race start. Consume 500-800 calories of easily digestible carbs, moderate protein, and low fat. Practice this meal in training. Sip on electrolyte drinks up until race start.

During

Swim: Nothing required. Bike: 60-90g carbs/hour, 24-30 oz fluid/hour, and 500-800mg sodium/hour. Run: 45-60g carbs/hour, adjusting based on temperature and individual needs. Alternate between solid food early and gels/liquids later.

Recovery

Immediate post-race: Fluid, sodium, and easy carbs. First 24 hours: Rehydrate with electrolytes, consume protein for muscle repair, and eat anti-inflammatory foods. Focus on sleep and active recovery in the following days.

Equipment Essentials

Swim

Wetsuit (appropriate for water temperature), goggles (bring a spare), tri suit, swim cap (race provided), anti-chafe lubricant, earplugs if sensitive to cold water.

Bike

Triathlon or road bike (properly fitted), cycling shoes, helmet, hydration system, spare tubes/CO2/tools, race nutrition, sunglasses, cycling computer to monitor effort.

Run

Running shoes (well broken-in), race belt, hat/visor, sunglasses, extra socks if desired, anti-chafe products, watch to track pace and distance.
