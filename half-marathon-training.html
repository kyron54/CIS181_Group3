The Race That Changes You

The half marathon (13.1 miles) is a mental and physical test. It's long enough to require serious preparation but achievable with dedication. Many runners say their first half marathon changed how they see themselves.

Finding Your Distance

The half marathon represents the perfect balance between the speed of shorter races and the endurance of a full marathon. It requires strategic training, mental preparation, and proper nutrition.

Our 10-14 week training plan builds both the physical and mental strength needed to conquer 13.1 miles and cross the finish line with a smile.

How to Train for a Half Marathon (10-14 Weeks Plan)

Your Weekly Structure

  • 4–5 runs per week
  • Long runs building up to 12 miles
  • Tempo runs (30-45 min at controlled hard pace)
  • Recovery runs to prevent burnout

Building Endurance

  1. Progressive Long Runs: Each week, add 1-1.5 miles to your long run.
  2. Tempo Runs: Sustained efforts at "comfortably hard" pace.
  3. Easy Recovery: Keep easy days truly easy to balance hard efforts.
  4. Fueling Practice: Use long runs to test race-day nutrition.

Mental Preparation

Miles 1-5

Keep it steady. The excitement will tempt you to go fast—don't. Conserve energy and find your rhythm. Many half marathons are lost in these early miles by going out too fast.

Miles 6-10

Settle into a mental rhythm. Lock into goal pace. Focus on form and breathing while maintaining a sustainable effort level. This is where the race truly begins.

Miles 11-13

This is the true test. Have a personal mantra ready to keep you going. Remember your training and know that the pain is temporary but your accomplishment will last forever.

Fueling for Success

Pre-race

Carbs the night before (pasta, rice, or potatoes). Morning of: Toast with honey, banana, or oatmeal 2-3 hours before start time. Hydrate well in the 24 hours before race day.

During

Energy gels or chews every 45 minutes. Most runners need 30-60g of carbs per hour during a half marathon. Drink water at every aid station, even if you don't feel thirsty.

Post-race

Protein + carbs to aid muscle repair. Aim for a 3:1 carb-to-protein ratio within 30 minutes of finishing. Don't forget to rehydrate with water and electrolytes.
