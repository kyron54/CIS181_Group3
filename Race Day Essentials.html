Race Day Essentials

Proper Running Shoes

Fit and comfort matter most. Shoes should be broken in but not worn out, chosen for your gait, arch, and terrain.

Moisture-Wicking Apparel

Stay dry and cool with performance fabrics. Avoid cotton to prevent chafing and help regulate body temperature.

Anti-Chafing Balm or Vaseline

Protect high-friction areas like inner thighs, underarms, and feet to prevent irritation during your run.

Running Socks

Choose cushioned, moisture-wicking socks to keep feet dry and prevent blisters. Avoid cotton!

Hydration Solution

Use a hydration belt, handheld bottle, or vest. Staying hydrated is key even in shorter races.

Fuel (For Longer Distances)

Energy gels, chews, or small snacks help maintain stamina, especially during 10Ks, half-marathons, and marathons.

Identification & Emergency

Carry an ID, emergency contact info, and medical alerts if needed. Safety first, always.

Pacing Strategy / Timing Device

Use a watch, app, or race-provided timing device to stay on track with your goal pace.
