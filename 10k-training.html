The Gateway to Distance Running

The 10K (6.2 miles) is twice the challenge of a 5K, requiring endurance and pacing discipline. It's the perfect stepping stone to longer distances while still allowing room for speedwork.

Building Distance with Speed

The 10K demands more endurance than a 5K but still rewards those with speed. Our training plan focuses on building a strong aerobic base while maintaining the ability to push the pace when needed.

This 8-10 week program will prepare both new 10K runners and those looking to improve their times with structured workouts that build endurance and speed.

How to Train for a 10K (8-10 Weeks Plan)

Your Weekly Structure

  • 4–5 runs per week
  • Long runs extending to 8 miles
  • Threshold runs at 80-90% effort
  • Race-pace intervals (e.g., 3 x 2 miles at goal pace)

Key Workouts

  1. Tempo Runs: 3-4 miles at a comfortably hard pace to build stamina.
  2. Progressive Long Runs: Start easy, finish at race pace.
  3. Interval Training: 800m and 1600m repeats to build speed and endurance.
  4. Recovery Runs: Easy pace to build mileage without adding stress.

Tactics for a Great Race

First Half

Negative Splits: The first 5K should be controlled—the second 5K is where you push. Start conservatively, aiming to run the second half faster than the first.

Hydration

Drink early in the race to avoid dehydration later. Even in a 10K, proper hydration can make the difference between a strong finish and hitting the wall.

Mental Focus

Mile 4 is where runners start doubting. Push past that wall. Have a mantra ready for when the race gets tough and your mind wants to give up before your body needs to.

What to Eat

Pre-race

Oatmeal with honey or a smoothie with banana and protein. Aim for 300-400 calories, easily digestible, 1.5-2 hours before the race.

During

Water at aid stations (unless extreme heat requires electrolytes). For most runners, a 10K doesn't require mid-race nutrition, just proper hydration.

Recovery

Chocolate milk or protein shake + whole grains. The 30-minute window after finishing is crucial for muscle recovery and glycogen replenishment.
