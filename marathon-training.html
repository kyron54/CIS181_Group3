The Ultimate Endurance Challenge

The marathon (26.2 miles) is the pinnacle of endurance running. It's a test of physical preparation, mental toughness, and race-day strategy.

Conquering 26.2 Miles

The marathon distance isn't just about running—it's about preparation, strategy, and mental fortitude. Our comprehensive training plan builds the endurance and resilience needed to cross the finish line.

This 12-20 week program is designed to gradually increase your mileage, build mental toughness, and teach you how to fuel properly for the ultimate running challenge.

How to Train for a Marathon (12-20 Weeks Plan)

Your Weekly Structure

  • 5–6 runs per week
  • Long runs extending to 20-22 miles
  • Marathon pace runs (10-12 miles at goal pace)
  • Speed and strength training to build resilience

Key Workouts

  1. Long Runs: The backbone of marathon training, building up to 20+ miles.
  2. Tempo Runs: Sustained efforts to build lactate threshold.
  3. Marathon Pace Work: Training your body to run efficiently at goal pace.
  4. Recovery Runs: Easy runs that build mileage without stress.

The Wall: Miles 18-20

Most marathoners hit a wall at mile 18-20. Why? Glycogen depletion. That's why fueling is crucial.

How to Break Through the Wall

  • Mental Preparation: Know the wall is coming and have strategies ready.
  • Proper Fueling: Take in carbs early and consistently throughout the race.
  • Pacing Strategy: Don't bank time early—it will cost you later.
  • Training Specificity: Incorporate 18-20 mile runs in training to teach your body to push past fatigue.

Fueling for the Marathon

Before the Race

Carb-load 2-3 days before (but don't overdo fiber). Increase carbohydrate intake to 70-80% of your diet in the 48-72 hours before race day. Maintain good hydration without overdrinking.

During the Race

30-60g carbs per hour (gels, chews, sports drinks). Start fueling early—don't wait until you feel hungry or tired. Hydrate early to prevent dehydration, taking in fluids at every aid station.

After the Race

Immediate protein (chicken, eggs, or protein shake) + carbs. Begin refueling within 30 minutes of finishing. Replace lost electrolytes and continue hydrating for 24-48 hours post-race.
